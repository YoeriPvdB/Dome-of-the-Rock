Temple Mount

In Judaism, the Dome of the Rock is significant for its position on Temple Mount, directly over the Foundation Stone and on the location where the First (Solomon’s) and Second (Herod’s) Temples stood. Temple Mount is so named for it being the location in the Old City of Jerusalem where both the First and Second Temples were built. It is the site of ancient Mount Moriah, which was built upon over the centuries to form Temple Mount.
First Temple: King Solomon, ~957 - 587 BC
Second Temple: King Herod, 516 BC - 70 AD
Third failed attempt: Roman Emperor Julian, 363 AD preceded the Dome’s construction by 300 years

Foundation Stone

In Jewish tradition, the Foundation Stone is one of the holiest sites on Earth. The 1.2 m x 0.72 m stone is said to be the location from which God began creation on the first day. It is also the location where He gathered the dust to create the first man, Adam, and believed to be where Abraham attempted to sacrifice his own son, Isaac, before God intervened. When the Temples stood, the Holy of Holies stood directly above the Foundation Stone. The Dome of the Rock currently stands over the Foundation Stone.

Holy of Holies

The Holy of Holies is the location inside the Tabernacle of the Temple where the Ark of the Covenant was kept, behind a veil to separate the area from the outside. It was said to be the location where the Divine Presence of God would reside on Earth. During the Jewish exodus from Egypt, the Tabernacle was a portable tent for housing the Holy of Holies and the Ark of the Covenant. It is now strongly correlated to the Foundation Stone due to the Temple’s location, as the Tabernacle was relocated permanently to the Temple when constructed, although where it was exactly located is unknown. For this reason, many Orthodox Jews do not ascend Temple Mount, to avoid potentially standing on the holiest of sites.
